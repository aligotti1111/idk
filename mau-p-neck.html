Mau P — "neck" Returns to Black Book Records With Minimal Tech House Fire

First introduced during his Sahara Tent headline at Coachella 2025, "neck" has been refined and road-tested for months. The track pairs Mau P's signature gritty basslines with playful vocal hooks in a rhythm-forward minimal tech house cut.

The Evolution

What started as a sought-after ID at Coachella has evolved through months of live refinement. The final release lands as a bold, sharpened take on the version that first caught global attention, reflecting the growing scale of Mau P's presence on the international stage.

The track is deliberately minimal, rude in its efficiency. It's the kind of record that works because of what it doesn't include as much as what it does.

Back to Black Book

This marks a return to Black Book Records, the label where Mau P made early waves with "Dress Code." That record helped cement his breakout, earning widespread DJ support and viral recognition, including a clip of Dua Lipa dancing to the track.

Since then, Mau P has expanded his presence with major milestones, including a high-production debut at Red Rocks Amphitheatre and a two-night, 40,000-ticket sellout at Los Angeles State Historic Park.

The Sound

The production is tight—funky percussion creating smooth, rolling momentum on the dancefloor, with disco-leaning synth stabs and vocal chops adding a final layer of polish. This is a highly functional, mix-ready cut with clear intent.

Looking Ahead

As he looks ahead to an ambitious 2026—including a debut Australian tour, major festival appearances at Ultra Buenos Aires, EDC Mexico, and Lightning in a Bottle, plus the launch of an album, label, and clothing line—"neck" feels deliberately timed.

It's a reminder of where Mau P came from and a clear signal of where he's headed: straight to the top of the tech house world.
