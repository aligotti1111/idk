FISHER — "Rain" Delivers Peak-Time Tech House Energy

By Milo Tress February 6, 2026 6 min read

FISHER

February 6, 2026

Australian powerhouse FISHER drops a dancefloor essential built around a rolling groove and hypnotic vocal hook. Released via his own imprint Catch & Release, "Rain" is exactly what you'd expect from the producer who's been voted the World's No. 1 House DJ—and then some.

The Sound

The track opens with a strutting bassline that nods subtly to disco influences while locking into an infectious groove. Meanwhile, the track's falsetto-leaning hook hangs in the air, gliding over the mix, awash in a sea of warm reverb. Tonally, it's bright without being saccharine, the kind of tune that quietly persuades you that your night has already improved just from having heard it.

This is FISHER at his most immediate and infectious. The production is deceptively simple—a testament to his ability to turn simplicity into pure impact. Every element serves the groove, and the groove serves the dancefloor.

The Live Test

"Rain" received its first major live moment at the history-making Moscone Center show in San Francisco, where 40,000 tickets were sold and the unreleased cut sent the crowd into an absolute frenzy. Since then, it's become a go-to weapon in his sets, signaling another defining chapter in his run of club-dominating releases.

The track has already earned its passport stamp, working its way into sets from Ibiza to Miami and everywhere in between. It's the kind of record that DJs love dropping when they want an instant reaction.

What's Next

FISHER shows no signs of slowing down in 2026. He's returning to Ibiza for year two of his Thursday night [UNVRS] residency from June 4 to September 10, building on a debut season that raised the bar for weekly residencies on the island. Outside the White Isle, highlights include Red Rocks Amphitheatre on May 23, his TRIIP Festival in Malta from May 28–31, and a highly anticipated UK headline show at Silverworks London on July 11.

The Verdict

"Rain" is FISHER doing what FISHER does best: creating peak-time anthems that work equally well in massive festival settings and intimate club environments. It's designed for long summer nights and hands-in-the-air moments, and it delivers on both fronts.

This one's built for the dancefloor, and it's not leaving anytime soon.
