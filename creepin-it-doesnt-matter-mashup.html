DJ Wish's mashup of The Weeknd's haunting vocals with SMACK's tech house production is a masterclass in balancing emotion with dancefloor energy. Part of the SoMa Mashup Pack that charted #6 on Hypeddit's Top 100, this blend proves that dark R&B and tech house belong together.

The Blend

The Weeknd's vocals from the track featuring 21 Savage and Metro Boomin carry an emotional weight that most tech house tracks lack. DJ Wish recognizes this and uses it to his advantage, layering the moody vocal over SMACK's driving four-on-the-floor groove.

The result is a track that works equally well at 2 AM in a warehouse or on a festival mainstage. The emotional resonance of The Weeknd's performance gives the mashup depth, while SMACK's relentless tech house energy keeps bodies moving.

Why It Works

The genius is in the restraint. DJ Wish doesn't over-process the vocals or try to force them into the beat. Instead, he lets The Weeknd's performance breathe while the tech house production builds around it. The tempos align naturally, and the dark, atmospheric quality of both tracks creates cohesion.

This isn't just two songs playing at the same time—it's a carefully crafted blend that respects both source materials while creating something entirely new.

The SoMa Pack Context

This mashup is one of 50 edits in DJ Wish's SoMa Mashup Pack Vol. 1, a collection that includes tech house, future house, bass house, and progressive house blends. The pack generated over 3,000 downloads and charted at #6 on Hypeddit's global Top 100.

DJ Wish has built a reputation for high-quality edits that DJs actually use in their sets. His approach prioritizes functionality over gimmicks, which is why his packs consistently chart and get heavy rotation.

The Verdict

This mashup is proof that tech house doesn't have to be one-dimensional. By bringing The Weeknd's emotional vocal performance into the club, DJ Wish creates a moment that resonates on both an emotional and physical level.

It's dark, it's driving, and it absolutely works.
