DJ Tennis teams up with rising vocalist Ashee for a hypnotic collaboration that bridges the gap between deep house and techno. Released via the legendary fabric Records imprint, "I Wanna Know" is a masterclass in restraint and atmosphere.

The Collaboration

DJ Tennis, the Italian producer and Life and Death label head, has built a reputation for sophisticated, emotionally resonant club music. His partnership with Ashee, whose ethereal vocals have been gaining traction in the underground, feels like a natural fit.

Ashee's voice floats over the track's stripped-back foundation, repeating the titular question with a sense of yearning that never tips into melodrama. It's intimate without being precious, emotional without being overwrought.

The Sound

The production is deceptively simple. A rolling bassline provides the backbone while carefully placed percussion creates a hypnotic groove that builds slowly over the track's runtime. Subtle pads and atmospheric textures fill the space between the vocals and the rhythm section.

This is music for 3 AM, when the dancefloor has thinned out but the energy hasn't dropped. It's deep, it's driving, and it demands attention without screaming for it.

fabric Records Legacy

Releasing on fabric Records carries weight. The label, connected to London's iconic fabric nightclub, has been a home for forward-thinking house and techno since 2000. DJ Tennis joining the roster feels like a statement about the label's continued commitment to pushing boundaries while respecting tradition.

The track fits perfectly into fabric's catalog—sophisticated enough for headphone listening, functional enough for peak-time club play.

Why It Works

What makes "I Wanna Know" special is its refusal to rush. In an era of instant gratification and TikTok-ready drops, DJ Tennis and Ashee have created something that unfolds slowly, rewarding patience and attention.

The track doesn't beg for your attention—it earns it through careful construction and emotional honesty. It's the kind of record that DJs will reach for when they want to shift the energy without breaking the spell.

The Verdict

"I Wanna Know" is proof that sometimes less really is more. DJ Tennis and Ashee have crafted a track that works equally well as a moment of introspection on the dancefloor or as the soundtrack to a late-night drive through empty city streets.

This is music for grown-ups—sophisticated, emotional, and built to last.
