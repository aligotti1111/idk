David Guetta & Chris Willis — Love is Gone (Olive Oil Remix)

Released Feb 11, 2026 Tech House Free Download

Olive Oil takes on David Guetta's 2007 classic "Love is Gone" and rebuilds it as a modern tech house weapon. Released as a Valentine's Day gift to DJs and dancefloors, this remix doesn't try to recapture the original's mid-2000s electro-house energy—instead, it translates the track's emotional core into contemporary club language that works in 2026.

Updating a Classic

The original "Love is Gone" was peak David Guetta era—vocal-driven house with crossover appeal that helped define what electro-house could be in the late 2000s. Chris Willis's vocals carried genuine emotion, and the production had that polished-yet-raw quality that made Guetta's early work essential.

Olive Oil's approach respects that foundation while completely rebuilding the sonic architecture. The Jersey-based producer specializes in the space where tech house meets techno, and that's exactly where this remix lives. The vocal moments are preserved but recontextualized—processed and layered to fit within a driving, groove-focused framework rather than sitting on top of big electro buildups.

The Production

This remix works because Olive Oil understands restraint. Rather than overloading the track with modern production tricks, the focus stays on groove and momentum. The bassline rolls consistently, percussion builds through subtle variations, and the vocals appear as carefully placed moments rather than constant presence.

It's tech house production that trusts the power of repetition and slow evolution—a clear contrast to the original's more dramatic arrangement. This version is built for extended club sets where DJs need tracks that maintain energy without demanding attention through obvious drops or breakdowns.

The Context

Olive Oil has built credibility through support from Kaskade, Alison Wonderland, Dom Dolla, Green Velvet, and Diplo—DJs who understand both underground credibility and festival effectiveness. That dual appeal shows in this remix, which could work equally well in a 2am warehouse or a festival techno tent.

Offering it as a free download shows smart positioning—get it into DJ boxes, let it prove itself on dancefloors, build momentum organically. The Valentine's Day release angle adds personality without being gimmicky; "Love is Gone" makes thematic sense for the occasion.

The Verdict

Olive Oil's "Love is Gone" remix succeeds at the difficult task of updating a classic without losing what made it resonate in the first place. This isn't nostalgia bait or a cheap festival edit—it's a thoughtful rework that brings a 2007 crossover hit into contemporary tech house context. The production is refined, the groove is consistent, and the vocal treatment respects the original while serving current dancefloor needs. For DJs looking for familiar material with modern energy, this delivers.
