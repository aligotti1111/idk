New Release / Progressive House

David Guetta, Kiko & Olivier Giacomotto — Prayer

Released Feb 20, 2026

David Guetta delivers "Prayer" alongside Kiko and Olivier Giacomotto, a progressive house collaboration that showcases the French superstar's continued exploration of deeper, more melodic territory. Released February 20, 2026, the track represents Guetta's ongoing evolution beyond his EDM-pop crossover era into underground-influenced progressive sounds.

The Collaboration

Kiko and Olivier Giacomotto bring technical production chops that complement Guetta's knack for melody and structure. This isn't a celebrity DJ slapping his name on someone else's work—it's a genuine collaboration where each producer's strengths enhance the final product. Giacomotto's underground credibility balances Guetta's mainstream sensibility, creating progressive house that works in both club and festival contexts.

"Prayer" features driving basslines, atmospheric pads, and melodic elements that build tension without resorting to the drop-heavy structure that defined Guetta's commercial peak. It's mature, considered production that respects progressive house traditions while incorporating modern sound design.

Guetta's Underground Turn

For over a decade, Guetta has been one of electronic music's biggest names, moving from underground French touch to global EDM superstardom. Recently, he's been recalibrating—releasing deeper material, collaborating with underground artists, and returning to the progressive house sound that informed his early career. "Prayer" fits within this trajectory, showing that commercial success doesn't preclude artistic evolution.

The Verdict

"Prayer" succeeds as progressive house that doesn't compromise for mass appeal. Guetta, Kiko, and Giacomotto create a track built for extended club sets and festival sunset slots—the kind of production that rewards patient listening and benefits from proper sound systems. For anyone who wrote off Guetta as purely commercial, this proves he's still capable of delivering underground-minded progressive house when he chooses to.
