Britney Spears — "I Wanna Go" (John Summit Remix) Is Peak Diva Energy on the Dancefloor

After months of teasing the track at festivals worldwide, John Summit has finally released his official remix of Britney Spears' 2011 dance-pop hit "I Wanna Go." And it's everything fans hoped it would be.

The Original Context

In 2011, Britney Spears released one of the most dance-forward songs of her career with "I Wanna Go." Written and produced by Max Martin and Shellback, the thumping Hi-NRG track hit No. 12 on the Global Dance Songs chart, where it spent 16 weeks. It was unapologetically fun, rebellious, and built for the dancefloor—exactly the kind of track ripe for a tech house reimagining.

The Long Tease

Summit has been playing this remix as an ID all summer—at Ultra Europe, Austin City Limits, and his own Experts Only festival. Every time it dropped, the crowd lost it. The anticipation built with each performance, creating massive demand for the official release.

The wait is finally over, and the official version delivers on every bit of that hype.

The Sound

Summit keeps much of Spears' original vocal intact, in moments chopping it up and adding spaces of near silence for dramatic effect. Then he dials up the BPM and lets the whole thing loose with a bouncy, festival-ready drop that's pure tech house fire.

The production is smart—Summit knows when to let Britney shine and when to build tension. The vocal chops are playful without being gimmicky, and the bassline drives forward with relentless energy. It's a feel-good club belter that breathes new life into the original by turning 2010s nostalgia into a contemporary dancefloor weapon.

Diva Energy

Summit himself celebrated the release by sharing a carousel of his "fav diva pics" from over the years, with the gallery featuring images of him in his performing and partying prime. "This is the energy u must channel while listening to this remix," he advises in the caption.

And he's not wrong. This track demands confidence, demands movement, demands hands in the air and zero inhibitions. It's peak diva energy translated into tech house—and it works perfectly.

The Timing

This remix caps off a busy run for Summit. The week before, he dropped the "Playa Package," a six-track EP of Burning Man-inspired remixes crafted specifically for his Mayan Warrior set. The man is on a tear, and each release feels more confident than the last.

Why It Works

The genius of this remix is that it doesn't try to be anything other than pure fun. Summit isn't making some deep artistic statement—he's making a track that works in clubs, at festivals, and in your headphones when you need an energy boost.

It respects the original while completely transforming it. Britney's pop sensibility meets Summit's tech house expertise, and the result is something that fans of both can appreciate.

The Verdict

John Summit's remix of "I Wanna Go" is exactly what it should be: an absolute blast. It's fun, it's energetic, and it's going to be inescapable on dancefloors for the foreseeable future.

Channel your inner diva and let this one rip.
