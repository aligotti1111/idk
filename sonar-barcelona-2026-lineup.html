Sónar Barcelona 2026 Unveils Stacked Lineup Led By The Prodigy & Charlotte De Witte

February 10, 2026Sónar4 min read

Since 1994, Sónar has positioned itself as the pulse of electronic music's current landscape. The Barcelona festival doesn't chase trends—it defines them. The 2026 lineup confirms that reputation remains intact.

The Headliners

The Prodigy returns to Sónar, bringing three decades of electronic punk energy. Charlotte De Witte and Amelie Lens represent techno's current dominance. Dom Dolla bridges underground credibility with festival-ready production. Boys Noize delivers his signature acid-tinged chaos. This isn't a lineup built on nostalgia or hype—it's a statement about electronic music's current creative centers.

Skepta's inclusion signals Sónar's continued interest in grime and UK bass culture. Kelis brings vocal power and crossover appeal. WhoMadeWho represents the intersection of live instrumentation and electronic production. Joy Orbison and Sammy Virji carry the UK's garage and bass traditions forward. Sara Landry pushes hard techno into new territories.

The Format

Sónar by Day features programming alongside the Sónar+D tech conference, creating space for both music discovery and industry dialogue. At night, the festival moves to a massive venue that transforms Barcelona into Spain's electronic music capital for one weekend. This day-night split has become Sónar's signature, allowing for contemplative daytime exploration and full-throttle nighttime release.

The Verdict

Sónar 2026 looks like what happens when a festival trusts its curatorial vision rather than chasing algorithms. The lineup works because it represents actual creative momentum in electronic music, not manufactured moments or legacy bookings. If you care about where electronic music is headed rather than where it's been, this is essential.
