New Release / Progressive House

deadmau5 ft. Stevie Appleton — Science

Released Feb 20, 2026 mau5trap

deadmau5 releases "Science" featuring Stevie Appleton via mau5trap, marking the official studio version of a track that's been devastating dancefloors since its debut at XS Nightclub Las Vegas in August 2025. The Canadian producer has road-tested this one extensively—from EDC Thailand to Rebel Toronto to Ultra Music Festival Argentina—refining the production through months of live performance before committing it to release.

The Road Test Method

"Science" first surfaced during deadmau5's Sunday MainStage set at XS on August 31, 2025. Since then, it's appeared in sets from Oliver Heldens, YOTTO, and deadmau5's techno alias testpilot, accumulating live play across progressive house, melodic techno, and mainstage contexts. This extended gestation period—six months from first play to official release—demonstrates deadmau5's approach to production: test it live, observe crowd response, make adjustments, repeat.

The track fits squarely within the progressive house framework deadmau5 helped define over two decades of production. Stevie Appleton's vocals provide melodic hooks without overwhelming the groove, allowing the production to maintain club functionality while offering radio accessibility. It's the balance deadmau5 has been navigating since "Strobe"—making tracks that work in both intimate club spaces and festival mainstages.

Stevie Appleton's Role

Appleton brings vocal performance that understands electronic music production constraints. His delivery sits within the mix rather than dominating it, providing enough melody to make the track memorable while leaving space for the synth work and bass progression that define deadmau5's sound. It's collaborative vocal work—recognizing that in progressive house, the voice is one element among many rather than the centerpiece.

The mau5trap Context

Released via deadmau5's own mau5trap imprint, "Science" benefits from the creative freedom that comes with self-releasing. No label pressure to chase radio formats, no forced features from trending vocalists, no compromises for streaming playlist consideration. deadmau5 can release what he wants, when he wants, after testing it as long as he needs. This autonomy shows in the final product—it's a track built for his sets first, broader audiences second.

mau5trap has become one of electronic music's most respected independent labels, releasing music from ATTLAS, No Mana, NERO, and Rinzen alongside deadmau5's own output. The label represents a specific sonic aesthetic—melodic without being saccharine, technical without being sterile, accessible without pandering. "Science" fits that framework perfectly.

The Live Circuit

By the time "Science" reached official release, it had already been played at EDC Thailand in January 2026, multiple testpilot sets, and various club shows across North America. This live-first approach means the track arrives with built-in momentum—DJs and crowds already know it works. The Spotify release formalizes what the dance music community has been experiencing for months.

The Verdict

"Science" succeeds because deadmau5 understands his strengths and doesn't try to be anything other than what he is—a producer who makes progressive house with melodic sensibility and technical precision. Stevie Appleton's vocals enhance rather than define the track, and six months of live testing ensures the final version works exactly as intended. For anyone who's been chasing IDs from deadmau5 sets since last summer, the official release delivers what you've been hearing. For everyone else, welcome to a masterclass in progressive house production from one of its architects.
