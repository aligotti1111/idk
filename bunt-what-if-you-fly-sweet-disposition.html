New Release / Stutter House

BUNT. & The Temper Trap — What If You Fly (Sweet Disposition)

Released Feb 13, 2026 Arista Records

BUNT. delivers his heartfelt interpretation of The Temper Trap's 2008 global phenomenon "Sweet Disposition," reimagining the beloved Australian rock anthem through his signature stutter house lens. "What If You Fly (Sweet Disposition)" arrives via Arista Records alongside news of his upcoming remix with Taylor Swift on "Opalite," marking a breakthrough moment for the "in the round" touring pioneer.

The Message Behind the Music

The track's title references a phrase BUNT. connects with deeply: "What if you fall? … But what if you don't? What if you fly?" It's a question he poses at the end of every show, encouraging audiences to embrace the unknown rather than fear it. For BUNT., this philosophy isn't abstract—it defined his 2025 breakthrough when he sold out Mexico City's Velódromo Olímpico stadium for 9,000 people early in his career. That risk became one of the defining moments of his life, and "What If You Fly" channels that same hopeful energy.

The original "Sweet Disposition" reached No. 8 on the UK Singles Chart in 2008 and has remained a cultural touchstone ever since. BUNT.'s stutter house approach honors the emotional weight of Dougy Mandagi and Lorenzo Sillitto's songwriting while introducing rhythmic stutters, filtered vocals, and production techniques that feel both nostalgic and forward-thinking. It's a rework that respects the source material rather than overwhelming it.

BUNT.'s Touring Empire

BUNT.'s ethos centers on bringing people together, and his touring schedule proves he means it. In 2025 alone, he played 156 shows across five continents, moving 150,000 tickets with signature pop-ups in NYC, LA, Mexico City, Hamburg, London, and Berlin. His "in the round" stage setup—inspired by traditional arena production but reimagined for electronic music—creates an immersive experience where no one is watching from a distance.

2026 is on track to surpass last year with 100+ dates including a 20,000-capacity venue in Cologne, 30,000 tickets in Munich, headline slots at Electric Love Festival in Austria and Under the K in New York, a Vegas residency at Wynn, and festival appearances at Coachella, Lollapalooza South America and India, and EDC Las Vegas. For an artist who surpassed 750 million streams and performed more than 350 shows globally, the momentum is undeniable.

The Catalog Behind the Success

"What If You Fly" follows collaborations with Elley Duhé, Tom Odell, The Chainsmokers, and Tiësto, solidifying BUNT.'s position among Spotify's "10 Dance Artists to Watch." His hit "Clouds" sits at over 450 million streams, and his catalog has surpassed 1 billion streams total. Rolling Stone x Sonos featured him discussing his humble road to success, dissecting the creative process behind "Clouds" and his approach to building community through music.

What began as a decade of quiet persistence has evolved into one of electronic music's most exciting breakout stories. BUNT.'s sound—stutter house with pop sensibility—occupies a unique space where festival main stages, intimate club shows, and streaming playlists all make sense. He's proven you can sell out stadiums while maintaining the intimacy that made underground dance music compelling in the first place.

Taylor Swift on the Horizon

The Taylor Swift "Opalite" remix announcement adds another layer to BUNT.'s trajectory. Following Chris Lake's official remix that hit #1 on iTunes, BUNT.'s version positions him alongside Skream, BUNT., and Elys Oaks in the limited CD run. It's a collaboration that bridges electronic music's underground roots with pop's commercial reach—exactly where BUNT. operates best.

The Verdict

"What If You Fly (Sweet Disposition)" succeeds because BUNT. understands what made the original resonate while trusting his production instincts enough to transform it. The stutter house treatment doesn't erase the song's emotional core—it amplifies it through rhythm and texture. For anyone who's been asking themselves "what if I fall?", BUNT.'s answer is clear: stop asking about the worst possible outcome and start imagining the best one. That mindset, applied to music production, touring, and life, is what separates artists who play it safe from those who fill stadiums.
